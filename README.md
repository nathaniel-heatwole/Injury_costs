SUMMARY

• Injury severity and economic cost modeling

• Published: Journal of Safety Research (2026), Injury Severity: Scales, Incidence, Hospitalization Rate, Mortality Risk, Economic Costs, Modeling Considerations, and Best Practices (https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0022437526000034)

• Quantified costs for the Injury Severity Score, which is ubiquitous in injury and safety research (12,000+ Google Scholar citations)

• Bounded feasible region, created probabilistic map, transferred existing cost data, and fit regression-based predictive and link models

• Developed models directly linking the Injury Severity Score to its parent scale, the Abbreviated Injury Scale (AIS)

• Cost data encompass: cost-of-injury (medical treatment, work lost), quality-related costs (e.g., pain/suffering), and willingness-to-pay (based on hedonic wage studies)

• Clustered ranges of injury severity values according to various data features, including the newly mapped cost values

• Methodology can be applied to any injury quantity (not just costs): incidence/prevalence, ED admission, hospitalization rate, length of stay, ICU admission, mortality rate, work days lost, and disability/impairment

• Allows seemingly disparate injury severity scales to be better and more directly compared and facilitates the pooling of mixed injury data in meta-analyses

• Keywords: Python, econometrics, health economics, operations research, model development/evaluation, logistic regression (ordinal, multinomial), log-log models, clustering (k-means), hypothesis tests (likelihood ratio test)
